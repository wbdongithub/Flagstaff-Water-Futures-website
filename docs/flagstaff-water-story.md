# Flagstaff's Water Story

Flagstaff has built a water system around local and regional groundwater, variable surface water from Upper Lake Mary, smaller Inner Basin supplies, conservation, and extensive reclaimed-water use. The system has served the community well, but it is exposed to wildfire, aging infrastructure, deep pumping costs, climate variability, and long project lead times.

The City's 2025 annual report states that 2024 potable production was 8,194 acre-feet: 61% aquifer groundwater, 36% Upper Lake Mary surface water, and about 2% Inner Basin water. Reclaimed production was 1,910 acre-feet and offset potable demand.

The future decision is not a declaration that today's system has failed. It is a question of what investment should come next—and how early Flagstaff must begin to reduce future risk.

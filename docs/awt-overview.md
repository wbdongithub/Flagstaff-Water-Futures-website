# Advanced Water Treatment

AWT adds multiple barriers beyond conventional wastewater treatment so reclaimed water can meet a defined potable-reuse goal. A Flagstaff train has not been selected. Candidate processes may include membrane or media filtration, ozone, biological activated carbon, granular or powdered activated carbon, UV, advanced oxidation, reverse osmosis, and final disinfection.

## Two missions

1. **Pathogen control**: achieve required log reductions through independent, validated barriers.
2. **Chemical control**: remove or destroy PFAS, trace organics, pharmaceuticals, endocrine-active compounds, industrial chemicals, and unknowns represented by surrogate monitoring.

## The salt issue

AWT does not eliminate the community salt balance. As reuse increases, dissolved salts can recirculate. A small first increment might be possible without full RO, but larger reuse may require desalting and a concentrate-management strategy.

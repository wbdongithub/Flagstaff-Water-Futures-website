# Water Quality and Trace Contaminants

Conventional wastewater treatment is highly effective for solids, oxygen-demanding material, nutrients, and pathogens when properly designed and operated. It was not designed to eliminate every modern trace chemical.

FWG is particularly interested in PFAS, pharmaceuticals, hormones and other endocrine-active compounds, personal-care products, pesticides, industrial chemicals, antibiotic-resistance concerns, and transformation products.

Evidence of human harm from low-level mixtures is difficult to establish. Evidence of biological effects in aquatic organisms is stronger for some compounds and exposure settings. The website will distinguish confirmed findings, plausible concerns, and unresolved questions as local documentation is added.

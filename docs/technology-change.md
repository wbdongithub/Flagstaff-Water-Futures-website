# When Should a Utility Reconsider an Old Conclusion?

Utilities are conservative for good reason: public-health systems must work every day. But mature industries can also resist change because staff are overloaded, existing designs are familiar, permits favor precedent, and large sunk planning efforts create momentum.

A disciplined re-evaluation does not chase every novelty. It asks whether a technology has crossed important thresholds: established regulations, multiple operating examples, reliable monitoring, qualified vendors and operators, known failure responses, and realistic financing. FWG believes AWT has changed enough to meet that test for renewed study.
